\begin{abstract}
This paper evaluates the Power Spectral Density (PSD) of random binary signals and real data sources in GNU Radio. The analysis includes bipolar rectangular pulses with different samples per symbol, white noise, image-based bit streams, and audio-based bit streams. Results show that increasing Sps improves time representation without changing the fundamental bandwidth, while real sources modify the spectral distribution due to their statistical structure. Additional pulse-shaping tests confirm the effect of line coding on DC content and spectral occupancy.
\end{abstract}
