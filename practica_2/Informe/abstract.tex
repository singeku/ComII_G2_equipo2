\begin{abstract}
This document analyzes the Power Spectral Density (PSD) of random signals and real-world data using GNU Radio. Random bit streams, images, and audio signals are evaluated to study their spectral behavior and the presence of DC components. The effect of the Samples per Symbol (Sps) parameter is examined, showing that it improves time resolution without modifying bandwidth. Additionally, interpolation filtering is applied to analyze line coding (NRZ, RZ, Manchester) and digital modulation techniques. The results highlight how signal structure and processing influence spectral characteristics and efficiency.
\end{abstract}
